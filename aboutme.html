Valentina Del Viscio

My father taught me how to use Windows 98 when I was 5 years old, and I spent my days drawing on Paint or sketching houses.

I also enjoyed creating photomontages, designing flyers, billboards, and stage sets for dance performances during my teenage years.

So when i grew up, enrolled in the Architecture Faculty in Ferrara, but during my Erasmus year in Valencia, I realized that architecture wasn't my calling.
I attended Visual Communication Design classes and felt completely immersed.

So, after completing my Master's degree in Architecture, I pursued a Master's degree in Digital Communication Design in Turin, where I discovered my true passion for UX/UI design.

Currently I work passionately as a UX/UI designer in a consulting company, where I embrace the opportunity to learn new things every day and better understand the needs of users.


- Despite spending a lot of time in front of screens, my vision is still 10/10
- I love gardening
- Iridescence is a property that stirs my soul
